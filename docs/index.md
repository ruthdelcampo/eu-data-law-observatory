# EU Data Law Observatory

An open research project tracking the European Union's growing body of data legislation — mapping fragmentation, overlaps, and conflicts across horizontal, sector-specific, and national data laws.

**[Read the Blog →](blog/)** — Analysis and proposals on the EU data law landscape

## The Problem

The EU has enacted a rapidly expanding set of data-related laws at multiple levels:

- **Horizontal regulations** that apply broadly: GDPR, Data Act, AI Act, Digital Services Act, Digital Markets Act
- **Sector-specific laws** targeting industries like finance (DORA, PSD2), health (EHDS), cybersecurity (NIS2, CRA)
- **National implementations** of EU directives, which vary across member states

These laws sometimes follow different principles, create overlapping obligations, and outright conflict with each other. The EU itself acknowledged this with the **Digital Omnibus** package in November 2025 — a proposal to harmonize definitions and reduce contradictions across its own digital legislation.

This observatory collects, archives, and analyzes this body of law to make the landscape navigable and the conflicts visible.

## Methodology

Each law goes through a collection pipeline:

1. **Fetch** the official text from [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/) in HTML format
2. **Convert** to clean, readable markdown
3. **Enrich** with structured metadata (dates, scope, relationships between laws)
4. **Analyze** for conflicts, overlaps, and compatibility issues

All legal texts are official documents of the European Union and are not subject to copyright.

## Roadmap
